We are a species that has spent 99.9% of its evolution in nature. Living in concrete boxes, under artificial lights, staring at glowing screens is a recent biological experiment. And the results on our mental health are not encouraging.

In Japan, doctors don't just prescribe pills. They prescribe **Shinrin-Yoku**, or "forest bathing". And it's not new-age philosophy, it's preventive medicine backed by decades of data.

### The Science of Green

What exactly happens when you enter a forest?

1.  **Cortisol Crash:** Studies conducted by Chiba University have shown that a 15-minute walk in the forest reduces salivary cortisol by 16%, blood pressure by 1.9%, and heart rate by 4% compared to a walk in the city. See also our article on [cortisol](/articoli/longevita/cortisolo-stress-cronico).
2.  **Immune Boost:** Trees release **phytoncides**, antimicrobial essential oils to protect themselves from insects. When we breathe them in, our body responds by increasing the activity of **Natural Killer (NK)** cells, which fight viruses and tumors. The effect lasts up to 30 days after a weekend trip.
3.  **Attention Reset:** Modern life requires "directed attention" (focus on screens, traffic, work), which depletes cognitive resources. Nature stimulates **"involuntary attention"** (soft fascination), allowing the prefrontal cortex to rest and regenerate.

### Rumination and Depression

A Stanford study scanned the brains of people after a 90-minute walk in nature vs. in an urban environment.
Those who had walked in nature showed reduced activity in the **subgenual prefrontal cortex**, the area associated with "rumination" (obsessive negative thinking about oneself), a key factor in depression.

Basically: nature turns off the "broken record" of negative thoughts.

### How to Practice Forest Bathing

You don't need to go to the Amazon. Here's how to do it correctly:

*   **Leave your phone at home (or in the car).** If you're making an Instagram story, you're not doing Shinrin-Yoku.
*   **Use your 5 senses.** Don't just look. Listen to the wind, touch the tree bark, smell the air (phytoncides!), taste (if you know what you're doing).
*   **Slow down.** The goal isn't to reach the top or do cardio. It's to be present. Walk at half your normal speed.
*   **Sit.** Find a comfortable spot and sit still for 15-20 minutes.

### Conclusion: Vitamin N

Consider time in nature not as a luxury, but as an essential nutrient: **Vitamin N**. If you feel anxious, mentally tired, or "stuck", the solution might not be another meditation app, but simply going outside.

Your brain recognizes nature as "home". Give it permission to return there every once in a while.
