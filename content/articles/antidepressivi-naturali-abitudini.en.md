Depression is not just "being sad." It is a complex physiological state where the nervous system is stuck in a "shutdown" mode. We often think the solution must come from the outside (a pill, therapy), but we forget that our body is a walking chemical pharmacy.

Every action you take sends a signal to your brain: "We are safe" or "We are in danger." Here are 5 powerful biological signals that act as natural antidepressants.

### 1. Morning Sunlight: Photonics
It's not poetry, it's neurobiology. Photons hitting the retina in the morning activate a neural circuit that stimulates the release of **serotonin** and **dopamine**.
*   **Protocol:** Get outside within 30-60 minutes of waking up. Stay out for 10-20 minutes (no sunglasses, but don't look directly at the sun). Light through a window is 50 times less powerful and isn't enough.

### 2. Movement: Behavioral Activation
When you're depressed, the last thing you want to do is move. But stillness signals to the brain that you are "sick" or "hibernating."
Physical exercise releases **endorphins** and **BDNF** (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor), a protein that repairs neurons damaged by stress.
*   **Protocol:** You don't need to run a marathon. A 30-minute brisk walk is enough to trigger neurogenesis. Do it even if you don't feel like it; action precedes motivation, not the other way around.

### 3. Regular Sleep: Neurochemical Reset
REM sleep is the brain's nightly therapy, where we process difficult emotions. Sleep deprivation amplifies the amygdala (the fear center) by 60%.
*   **Protocol:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time. Total darkness and a cool temperature (18-19°C) are non-negotiable. See our guide on [sleep](/articoli/sonno/12-consigli-pratici-sonno-insonnia).

### 4. Social Connection: Oxytocin
Isolation is both a symptom and a cause of depression. We are social primates; our nervous system is wired for co-regulation.
*   **Protocol:** Seek eye and physical contact (even a handshake or a hug). You don't need a party; an authentic conversation with a friend or family member releases oxytocin, the natural antidote to cortisol.

### 5. Omega-3 and Nutrition: Inflammation
There is a strong correlation between systemic inflammation and depression (the "inflammatory theory of depression").
*   **Protocol:** Reduce sugars and processed foods that cause inflammation. Increase intake of EPA (a type of Omega-3). Studies suggest that 1-2 grams of EPA per day can have significant antidepressant effects. See our [supplement guide](/articoli/nutrizione/guida-integratori).

### Conclusion
You don't have to do everything at once. Choose one of these habits and apply it tomorrow. You are sending your brain a new message: "We are moving, we are seeing the light, we are healing."
