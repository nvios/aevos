Magnesium is the "master mineral." It's a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions, including ATP (energy) production, DNA synthesis, and nervous system regulation.
Unfortunately, modern soils are depleted, and vegetables contain less magnesium than 50 years ago. Moreover, [stress](/articoli/longevita/cortisolo-stress-cronico) and sugar consume the body's magnesium reserves at record speed.

### What It Really Does
*   **Relaxation:** Acts as a natural brake for the nervous system, countering excessive excitation. Essential for those who suffer from anxiety or insomnia.
*   **Sleep:** Regulates melatonin and GABA.
*   **Heart:** Helps maintain stable heart rhythm and blood pressure under control.
*   **Muscles:** Prevents cramps and muscle tension.

### Guide to Chemical Forms
Not all magnesium supplements are equal. The magnesium atom must be bound to a carrier to be absorbed.
1.  **Magnesium Bisglycinate:** Bound to the amino acid glycine. Very high bioavailability, very calming, zero laxative effect. **Ideal for sleep.**
2.  **Magnesium Citrate:** Bound to citric acid. Good absorption, but may have a slight laxative effect. Useful if you also suffer from constipation.
3.  **Magnesium L-Threonate:** The only one capable of effectively crossing the blood-brain barrier. Studied for memory and cognitive function (more expensive).
4.  **Magnesium Malate:** Bound to malic acid. Excellent for muscle energy and fibromyalgia.

### Supplementation Strategy
Start with 200-400mg of Magnesium Bisglycinate in the evening. It's one of the safest [supplementation strategies](/articoli/nutrizione/guida-integratori) with the best cost/benefit ratio for improving immediate quality of life.
As always, consult your doctor, especially if you have kidney or heart problems.
