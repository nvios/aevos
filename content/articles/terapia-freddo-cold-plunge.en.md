Wim Hof made ice famous, but the benefits of cold go beyond breathing. Deliberate cold exposure is acute stress that triggers powerful hormonal responses.

### 1. The Dopamine Surge (Focus and Mood)
Immersion in cold water increases dopamine (the motivation hormone) by 250%. Unlike drugs or sugar, this increase is not a spike followed by a crash, but a sustained elevation that lasts for hours.
**Result:** Laser mental focus and improved mood all day long.

### 2. Brown Fat Activation (Metabolism)
Brown adipose tissue (BAT) is a type of fat that burns calories to generate heat (thermogenesis). Cold activates it and stimulates its growth.
The more brown fat you have, the better your [insulin](/articoli/longevita/insulina-resistenza-metabolismo) sensitivity and basal metabolism.

### 3. Immune System
Thermal shock temporarily increases white blood cells and reduces systemic chronic inflammation. It's "training" for the immune system.

### Base Protocol
*   **Temperature:** 10-15°C to start.
*   **Duration:** 1-3 minutes. No need to stay in for 10 minutes (hypothermia risk).
*   **Frequency:** 11 total minutes per week (split into 2-4 sessions) appears to be the minimum effective dose.

**Warning:** Cold is a powerful stressor. If you're already exhausted or sick, it can make things worse. Listen to your body.
