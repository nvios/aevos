In the world of longevity, there is a "dirty secret" that few want to admit: what makes you big and strong in the gym might be exactly what shortens your theoretical maximum lifespan.

Welcome to the paradox of **mTOR** and **IGF-1**.

These two molecules are the "generals" commanding your body's growth. Nature designed them to make you grow fast and reproduce. But nature doesn't care if you live to 90. For longevity, uncontrolled growth is the enemy.

### The Protagonists: mTOR and IGF-1

To understand how to "hack" aging, you must know the players:

1.  **mTOR (mechanistic Target Of Rapamycin):** It is your cells' nutrient sensor. When you eat protein (especially the amino acid leucine), mTOR lights up and screams: "We have resources! Let's build new cells! Let's make muscles!".
    *   **The Good Side:** Builds muscle, repairs tissues, makes you strong.
    *   **The Bad Side:** When active, it blocks **autophagy** (cellular cleanup). If mTOR is always on, cells accumulate junk and cancer risk increases.

2.  **IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1):** It is the hormone that promotes cell division.
    *   **The Case of Laron Dwarfs:** There is a population in Ecuador with a mutation keeping IGF-1 extremely low. These people are very short, but are almost immune to cancer and diabetes. They live long, despite imperfect lifestyles.

### The Dilemma: Sarcopenia vs. Cancer

Here lies the practical problem.
*   To maximize **Lifespan**, you would want low mTOR and IGF-1 to reduce cancer and metabolic disease risk.
*   To maximize **Healthspan**, you need muscle to avoid [sarcopenia](/articoli/esercizio/sarcopenia-forza-over-50) and fractures, which requires high mTOR and IGF-1.

If you eat little protein, you will live long but be frail and might die from a fall at 80.
If you eat protein like a bodybuilder 24/7, you will be very strong but might accelerate cellular aging.

### The Practical Solution: The Switch and Cycling

The answer is not "all or nothing," but **when**. You must treat mTOR like a light switch, not a stuck dimmer.

#### 1. The "Pulse & Fast" Strategy
Don't keep mTOR always on (eating protein every 3 hours) nor always off (extreme low-protein veganism).
*   **Growth Phase (Pulse):** When you weight train, eat a high dose of protein (30-40g) immediately after. This activates mTOR *locally* in muscles for repair, which is what we want.
*   **Repair Phase (Fast):** Away from workouts, or on specific days, reduce protein or practice [intermittent fasting](/articoli/nutrizione/digiuno-intermittente-autofagia). This turns off mTOR and allows autophagy.

#### 2. Protein: Quality and Timing
*   You don't need protein at breakfast, lunch, and dinner if you are sedentary.
*   Concentrate protein around physical activity.
*   Plant proteins stimulate less mTOR than animal ones (they have less leucine). A predominantly plant-based diet with targeted "incursions" of animal protein (or supplements) post-workout is an excellent compromise for longevity.

#### 3. The Age Factor: A Crucial Element
The strategy changes with years:
*   **Under 65:** The body is very efficient at growing. The biggest risk is hyper-proliferation (cancer). **Strategy:** Keep protein moderate (0.8 - 1.0g per kg), low IGF-1. Do cycles of [Fasting Mimicking Diet](/articoli/nutrizione/dieta-mima-digiuno-fmd).
*   **Over 65:** The body becomes resistant to growth signals (anabolic resistance). The biggest risk becomes frailty. **Strategy:** Increase protein (1.2 - 1.5g per kg). Now sarcopenia risk outweighs the theoretical risk of slightly higher IGF-1.

### Conclusion

You don't have to choose between being a bodybuilder or a fasting monk.
Use protein as a technology: consume it strategically to build the "machine body" you need to move, but allow ample periods of "simulated famine" to let your body clean and regenerate itself.
Dynamic balance is the true key to biological immortality.
