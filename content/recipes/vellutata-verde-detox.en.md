ingredients:
  - 400g Zucchini
  - 200g Fresh Spinach
  - 1 Leek (white part only)
  - 1 clove Garlic
  - 500ml Vegetable Broth (homemade or organic low sodium)
  - 1 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil (raw)
  - 1 tsp Turmeric powder
  - Black Pepper to taste
  - Pumpkin Seeds (optional, for garnish)
instructions:
  - >-
    Wash the zucchini thoroughly and slice into rounds. Clean the leek and slice
    thinly.
  - >-
    In a pot, sauté the leek with a tablespoon of water (no oil to keep cooking
    calories low).
  - >-
    Add the zucchini and crushed garlic. Cover with hot vegetable broth.
  - >-
    Bring to a boil and cook for about 15 minutes until zucchini is tender.
  - >-
    Add fresh spinach and turmeric. Cook for another 2-3 minutes (spinach just
    needs to wilt).
  - >-
    Turn off heat. Blend everything with an immersion blender until smooth and
    creamy.
  - >-
    Serve hot with a drizzle of raw EVOO and freshly ground black pepper
    (essential to activate turmeric).

This soup is specifically designed for those following calorie restriction protocols like the **Fasting Mimicking Diet** or **Intermittent Fasting**.

It's a micronutrient powerhouse with minimal caloric impact. Zucchini provides volume and potassium, while spinach adds iron, magnesium, and B vitamins.

### The Functional Touch: Turmeric & Pepper
We added turmeric not just for color, but for its potent anti-inflammatory action. Always remember to add a pinch of black pepper: piperine increases curcumin absorption by up to 2000%.

Consume it for dinner to promote restful sleep and avoid burdening nighttime digestion.
